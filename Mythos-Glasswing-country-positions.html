Official Country & Jurisdiction Positions on Anthropic's Mythos and Project GlasswingAll URLs & quotes verified

A compiled register of public statements, regulatory engagements, and ministerial reactions to Claude Mythos Preview — the frontier model Anthropic has restricted from broad release over its autonomous cyber-offensive capabilities — and the associated Project Glasswing partner programme.

Compiled 16 April 2026  ·  Each source URL individually fetched and confirmed live  ·  Every quote cross-checked against the cited page as verbatim

Context

Claude Mythos Preview — unveiled by Anthropic on 7 April 2026 — is an unreleased frontier model that Anthropic and the UK AI Security Institute describe as the most capable AI system yet evaluated on offensive cyber tasks, capable of autonomously identifying and chaining software vulnerabilities and completing full multi-step corporate network intrusions.

Project Glasswing is Anthropic's response: a restricted-access initiative giving roughly 12 lead partners (Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Cisco, JPMorgan Chase, Nvidia and others) and ~40 additional critical-infrastructure organisations defensive-only access to the model, backed by USD 100M in usage credits and USD 4M in open-source security donations.

Governments have split broadly into four camps: (i) those with early testing access (UK); (ii) those in active dialogue without access (Germany, Canada, EU institutions); (iii) those reviewing published material and issuing public guidance without access (Ireland, Netherlands, South Korea); and (iv) those largely sidelined (most EU member states).

Scope. The table captures jurisdictions for which an identifiable government official, regulator, central bank, or national cyber agency has publicly stated a position, entered formal dialogue with Anthropic, or issued official guidance. Private-sector quotes (bank CEOs, partner firms) are excluded. The India row is labelled as opinion commentary and is included for policy context; the Government of India has not yet issued an official statement.

Jurisdictions confirmed sidelined without quotable positions (per Politico's 14 April survey of eight continental-European cyber agencies): France (ANSSI), Italy, Spain, Poland, Sweden, Belgium — contacted but without public statements of record at time of compilation. China has issued no public government statement; its position has been referenced only indirectly through US officials' framing of Mythos as a US–China AI-race asset. Japan and Australia have been cited by media commentary but without on-record ministerial or agency statements specific to Mythos.

Rapidly evolving. The Mythos disclosure is less than two weeks old. This register should be treated as a snapshot at 16 April 2026; additional ministerial statements, AI-safety-institute evaluations, and parliamentary inquiries are expected in the coming weeks.
